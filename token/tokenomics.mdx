### How It Works

The OURO Vault is a straightforward staking system. You lock OURO tokens for a chosen duration and earn weighted staker points based on your lock commitment.

### Lock Durations & Multipliers

| Lock Duration | Multiplier | Best For |
|---------------|-----------|----------|
| No lock (flexible) | 1x | Testing the waters |
| 7 days | Higher | Short-term commitment |
| 30 days | Higher | Medium-term stakers |
| 60 days | Highest | Long-term believers |

<Info>
Longer locks earn more points per token staked. The multiplier rewards patience and commitment to the ecosystem.
</Info>

### Staking Details

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Minimum Stake | 100 OURO |
| Lock Options | Flexible, 7 days, 30 days, 60 days |
| Withdrawal | After lock period ends |
| Points | Weighted by amount and lock multiplier |

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do staker points work?" icon="chart-line">
    Your staker points are calculated as your staked amount multiplied by your lock multiplier. Longer locks mean more points, which translates to a larger share of rewards.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I have multiple positions?" icon="list">
    Yes. You can create multiple staking positions with different lock durations. Each position is tracked separately.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens when my lock expires?" icon="unlock">
    Once your lock period ends, you can withdraw your full OURO amount at any time. There are no penalties for withdrawing after the lock ends.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is my OURO safe?" icon="shield">
    Your tokens are held in a smart contract vault. Only you can withdraw your positions. Not even the Slinq team can access your staked tokens.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Buyback & Burn

A portion of protocol fees are used to **buy back SLINQ** from the market and **permanently burn** it.

| Source | Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Trading Fees (Protocol Share) | Buyback SLINQ via PancakeSwap | Reduces circulating supply |
| LP Fee Harvesting | Token side burned, BNB side → buyback | Reduces supply of both tokens |
| Deflationary Pressure | Continuous burns | Increasing scarcity |
