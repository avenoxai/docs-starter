# Trading Fees Explained

Slinq supports two fee models. Ethereum launches use Slinq's Clanker wrapper and show creator-facing revenue in ETH terms. BNB Chain Infinity launches use a PancakeSwap hook with a 1% base fee plus dynamic launch and volatility protection.

## Ethereum vs BNB Chain Fees

| Chain | How fees work |
|-------|---------------|
| **Ethereum** | Launches route through the Slinq Clanker wrapper. Creator-facing revenue combines the creator and initializer-side revenue Slinq exposes to the creator dashboard. |
| **BNB Chain Infinity** | A PancakeSwap hook collects a 1% base fee, can add launch/volatility premiums up to a 5% cap, and splits fees between creator, initializer, and protocol. |
| **BNB Chain V3** | No hooks. Creator rewards come from harvested LP fees on the locked V3 position. |

The sections below explain the BNB Chain Infinity hook model.

## What Are Hooks?

Hooks are PancakeSwap V4's way of letting protocols like Slinq add custom logic to every trade. Think of them as an automatic middleman that:

- **Calculates the right fee** based on market conditions
- **Protects against bots** during the first 30 seconds of a launch
- **Splits fees** between the token creator and the protocol
- **Adjusts for volatility** to protect against manipulation

You don't need to do anything — hooks work invisibly in the background on every trade.

## Fee Beneficiaries

When you launch your own token, you receive the full **40% creator share**. But when someone else helps bring your token to life (for example, through [social tokenization](/create/social-tokenization)), the fee split adjusts:

| Scenario | Creator | First Buyer | Protocol |
|----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **You launch your own token** | 40% | — | 60% |
| **Someone else triggers your launch** | 35% | 5% | 60% |

The "first buyer" is the person who actually executes the launch transaction on-chain. They receive a 5% share as a reward for putting your token live, while you still earn 35% as the creator.

<Tip>
Both scenarios give creators significant ongoing income. The 5% first-buyer reward incentivizes people to launch tokens they discover, creating a healthy ecosystem.
</Tip>

## Two Types of Fees

Slinq has two separate fee mechanisms that work together:

### 1. Hook Fees (Trading Fees)

The **1% base fee** (plus any dynamic premiums) is the **hook fee** — collected by the hook on every trade and distributed between stakeholders:

| Recipient | Share | Purpose |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Token Creator** | 40% (or 35%) | Rewards the token creator |
| **First Buyer** | 0% (or 5%) | Rewards the person who launched the token |
| **Slinq Protocol** | 60% | Platform sustainability |

<Info>
Hook fees are what you see deducted from your trades. These go to people, not burns.
</Info>

### 2. LP Fees (Liquidity Provider Fees)

Separately, PancakeSwap's native **LP fees** (0.3%) accrue to the locked liquidity position. Slinq uses these LP fees to create **deflationary pressure**:

  </Card>
  <Card title="BNB Side → $SLINQ Buyback" icon="rotate" color="#4ECDC4">
    The BNB portion of LP fees is used to **buy back and burn $SLINQ** on a regular basis, creating sustained deflationary pressure on the protocol token.
  </Card>

<Tip>
Every trade on Slinq contributes to deflation through LP fees - both for the token being traded AND for $SLINQ itself.
</Tip>

## MEV Protection Fees

During the first 30 seconds after launch, a premium is added to **buy orders only** to protect against sniper bots. The premium decays using a **parabolic (x²) curve**, and the total hook fee is capped at **5%**:

| Time After Launch | Buy Fee | Sell Fee |
|-------------------|---------|----------|
| 0 seconds | 5% | 1% |
| 10 seconds | ~3.2% | 1% |
| 20 seconds | ~1.6% | 1% |
| 30 seconds+ | 1% | 1% |

### The Math Behind MEV Protection

The MEV premium follows a parabolic decay formula:

<Card title="MEV Premium Formula" icon="function">
```
mevPremium = 5% × (timeRemaining / 30)²

totalBuyFee = min(5%, baseFee + mevPremium)
           = min(5%, 1% + 5% × (timeRemaining / 30)²)
```

Where `timeRemaining` = 30 seconds - elapsed time since launch
</Card>

**Example calculations:**
- At launch (t=0): `min(5%, 1% + 5% × (30/30)²) = 5%`
- At 10 seconds: `1% + 5% × (20/30)² = 1% + 2.22% = 3.22%`
- At 20 seconds: `1% + 5% × (10/30)² = 1% + 0.56% = 1.56%`

### Why MEV Protection?

MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) bots try to:
- **Front-run** your transactions to buy before you
- **Sandwich** your trades to profit from price movement
- **Snipe** new launches in the first blocks

High early fees make these attacks unprofitable, giving regular users a fair chance.

<Tip>
If you're not in a rush, waiting 30 seconds after launch means you pay standard 1% fees.
</Tip>

## Volatility Fees

During periods of high price volatility, fees increase to protect liquidity providers. This uses a **Volatility Accumulator (VA)** that tracks price movements.

| Volatility Level | Fee Range | When It Triggers |
|------------------|-----------|------------------|
| **Normal** | 1% | Typical trading conditions |
| **Elevated** | 1-3% | Moderate price swings |
| **High** | 3-5% | Significant price movement |

<Warning>
The maximum total hook fee is capped at **5%** regardless of how volatile the market becomes.
</Warning>

### The Math Behind Volatility Fees

Volatility fees use a **VA² (Volatility Accumulator Squared)** formula inspired by Meteora's DLMM:

<Card title="Volatility Fee Formula" icon="function">
```
volatilityPremium = (N × VA²) / 1,000,000

Where:
- VA = Volatility Accumulator (tracks tick deviations)
- N  = Fee Control Numerator (default: 40,000)

totalFee = max(mevFee, baseFee + volatilityPremium)
```

The VA accumulates based on how far the price moves from a reference point, then decays over time.
</Card>

**How the VA works:**
1. **Accumulation**: When price moves, VA increases by the tick deviation
2. **Decay**: If no trades occur, VA decays by 50% after 60 seconds
3. **Reset**: After 4 minutes of stability, VA resets to 0

**Example calculations:**
- VA = 500 (5% price move): `40,000 × 500² / 1,000,000 = 1% premium`
- VA = 1000 (10% move): `40,000 × 1000² / 1,000,000 = 4% premium`
- VA = 1500 (15% move): `40,000 × 1500² / 1,000,000 = 9%` → capped at 5%

<Info>
The VA² formula means small moves have minimal impact, but large rapid moves trigger significant protection for LPs.
</Info>

## Fee FAQ

<Accordion title="Do I pay fees on both buys and sells?">
  Yes, the base 1% fee applies to every trade. However, the MEV premium (during the first 30 seconds) only applies to **buy orders** - sells always pay the standard fee.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are fees included in the quote?">
  Yes. The amount shown as "You Receive" is after all fees are deducted.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I avoid MEV fees?">
  Wait 30 seconds after launch to pay standard 1% fees. The MEV decay is automatic. Or sell instead of buy - MEV fees only apply to buys.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What's the maximum fee I could ever pay?">
  The absolute maximum hook fee is **5%**. Under normal conditions, you'll usually pay close to the 1% base fee.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Where can I see my fee contributions?">
  Visit the token page to see:
  - Total fees collected
  - Your contribution to trading volume
  - Creator earnings from your trades
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="As a creator, how do I claim fees?">
  Visit your creator dashboard and click "Claim" to withdraw accumulated fees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does the token burn work?">
  This applies to **LP fees** (PancakeSwap's 0.3%), not hook fees. LP fees accumulate in both the token and BNB. The **token side is burned** directly, reducing supply. The **BNB side** funds regular $SLINQ buybacks and burns. Every trade makes tokens more scarce.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What's the difference between hook fees and LP fees?">
  **Hook fees** (1%+) go to creators, pool initializers, and the protocol - these are the fees you see in your trade quotes. **LP fees** (0.3%) are PancakeSwap's native fees that accrue to liquidity - Slinq uses these for token burns and $SLINQ buybacks.
</Accordion>
