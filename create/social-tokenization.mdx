# Social Tokenization

See a tweet or cast going viral? You can turn it into a tradeable token just by replying with a keyword. The original author automatically becomes the token creator and earns creator revenue from every trade.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Spot a great post">
    Find a tweet or Farcaster cast you think deserves a token.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reply with a keyword">
    Reply to the post mentioning `@slinqmeme` (Twitter) or `@slinq` (Farcaster) with one of the trigger keywords.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI generates the token">
    Slinq's AI reads the original post and automatically generates a fitting token name, ticker, and description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Token goes live">
    The token deploys with the **original author** as the creator. Social tokenization defaults to Ethereum through Slinq's Clanker wrapper unless BNB Chain is explicitly requested.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Trigger Keywords

Reply to any post with one of these keywords (plus the bot mention):

```
tokenize, tokenise, make this a token, coin this, launch this, mint this,
turn this into a token, token this
```

**Example reply on Twitter:**
```
@slinqmeme tokenize this
```

**Example reply on Farcaster:**
```
@slinq coin this
```

<Tip>
The keywords are flexible and work in multiple languages (including Chinese). As long as your reply contains one of the trigger phrases and mentions the bot, it will work.
</Tip>

## Who Becomes the Creator?

This is the key difference from a normal launch:

| Launch Method | Who is the Creator | Who Earns Fees |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Social Tokenization** (reply) | Original post author | Original post author |
| **Normal Launch** (direct request) | The person requesting | The person requesting |

<Info>
The person who replies with "tokenize this" is **not** the creator. The author of the original post is. This means you can gift someone a token and they'll earn from it.
</Info>

## Token Parameters

Socially tokenized posts use these settings by default:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Launch Chain | Ethereum |
| Starting FDV | 2 ETH |
| Total Supply | 100 Billion |
| Name & Ticker | AI-generated from the post content |
| Description | AI-generated summary of the post |
| Image | Auto-generated |

<Info>
Ask for BNB Chain explicitly if you want the PancakeSwap launch rail instead.
</Info>

## Where Do These Tokens Appear?

Socially tokenized tokens appear on the **Social Feed** page at [slinq.meme/socialfeed](https://slinq.meme/socialfeed). This is a dedicated feed showing tokens born from social posts, separate from the main homepage.

## AI-Powered Token Creation

When you trigger a social tokenization, Slinq's AI:

1. **Reads the original post** to understand the context
2. **Generates a token name** that captures the essence of the post
3. **Creates a ticker** that's catchy and memorable
4. **Writes a description** based on the post's content
5. **Replies with a confirmation** including the trading link

You don't need to specify any parameters — the AI handles everything.

## Fee Sharing

When someone else tokenizes your post, you earn as the creator. On BNB Chain hook launches, the creator/initializer split is:

| Scenario | Creator Share | First Buyer Share | Protocol |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| You tokenize your own post | 40% | — | 60% |
| Someone else tokenizes your post | 35% | 5% | 60% |

<Info>
If someone else triggers the launch of your token (they're the "first buyer"), they receive a 5% share of trading fees as a reward. You still get 35% as the creator.
</Info>

## Common Questions

<Accordion title="What if the original author doesn't have a wallet?">
  The token is created and fees accumulate. When the author connects a wallet to Slinq (via their Twitter or Farcaster account), they can claim all accumulated fees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I tokenize my own post?">
  Yes! Reply to your own tweet or cast with a trigger keyword. You'll be both the author and the creator.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if the post has already been tokenized?">
  Each post can only be tokenized once. If someone already tokenized it, you'll get a reply letting you know.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can the original author reject the token?">
  Tokens are permissionless — once created, they exist on the blockchain permanently. However, the author can choose not to claim their fees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does the author need to do anything?">
  Nothing. Fees accumulate automatically. The author just needs to connect their wallet to Slinq whenever they want to claim.
</Accordion>
